Carry Over Damage

Carry over damage is damage done after the original layer of a cog has been defeated. Only damage dealt after an entire cog's original layer is destoryed is applied to the skelecog underneath in the current round. This tool should help aid you in finding how much damage to deal in the next round. Defeating the first layer on a cog will cause the cog to not attack after all gags have been used.

To give an example, let's take a level 12 2.0 cog. Let's say in the first round one toon uses some type of lure and three toons use a maxed storm cloud. Assuming the lure hits, the toons end up hitting a total of 408 damage. A 2.0 cog has 400 health (double the health of a level 12 1.0 cog). So you would think that the cog would be completely destroyed. However, 240 damage was applied to the first layer while only 168 damage was applied to the second layer. Since a maxed storm cloud hits 80 we do 80 * 3 = 240 damage (80 damage for each storm cloud times 3 clouds used; the red number that pops up). This destroys the first layer of the cog without carrying over the remaining 40 damage. We then apply the track bonus damage of 20% (the yellow number that pops up) which turns out to be 48. Afterwards, we then apply the lured bonus damage of 50% of the total storm clouds which ends up to be 120 damage. 48 + 120 = 168 damage. Therefore, the first layer of the cog is destroyed, so the cog won't attack at the end of the round, but the cog will still have 32 HP (200-168) remaining. Note: The lured bonus damage is put into the calculation after the track bonus damage even though it visually appears before.
